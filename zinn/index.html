Fun Facts About Bread

Here are some things about bread that you probably didn't know!

  1. Bread used to be so important in Egypt that it was used as money!
  2. On average, Americans eat 53 pounds of bread per year. (each!)
  3. Before rubber erasers, people would use small balls of white bread to erase graphite.

Different Types of Bread

There are over 100 different varieties of bread in the world today! Many of these can be put into sub-categories based on their ingredients or shape, like white, wheat, or flatbread. Different types of bread come from different regions of the world. A certain style of bread can arise simply because of what was available when people started making it. For example, challah comes from a region where oil is more widely used than butter, making it different from brioche. Here are some examples of those sub-categories:

  • White: a bread made from flour that has been heavily processed
  • Wheat: a bread with a bit more nutrients than white, but still made with refined white flour.
  • Whole Grain: a bread made with grains that remain fully intact, such as wheat, barley, and more.
  • Flatbread: a mixture of flour, water and salt, rolled flat and cooked. Some, like pita, may also include yeast.
  • Sourdough: bread that achieves rise from fermentation instead of yeast. Any kind of bread can be sourdough, but the fermentation gives a slightly more sour taste than yeast.
  • Quick Bread: a type of bread that gets its rise from a chemical leavener such as baking powder or baking soda instead of from yeast or fermentation.

So Why Should You Eat Bread?

It's good for you!

Bread, especially that of the whole grain variety, contains a lot of important nutrients. Some kinds are fortified with calcium and iron, essential for maintaining healthy immune systems, energy, and bones. Bread is also a good source of fiber, which helps regulate our digestive system, keep blood sugar and cholesterol in check, and makes us feel full for longer.

Most importantly, it's delicious!
