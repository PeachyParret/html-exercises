High Intensity Interval Training Workouts

The Ladder

This effective workout can be done in less than an hour and leave you sore for days

For the first set complete 8 of the following and each time you repeat, decrease your rep by one:

  • Shoulder taps
  • Burpees
  • Butterfly situps
  • Jump Squats
  • Push-ups
  • 2 minute sprint
  • 45 second rest

For the second set complete 45 seconds of each and repeat 3 times

  • Reverse lunges
  • Jumping Jacks
  • Jump squats
  • Mountain climbers
  • Shoulder taps
  • 15 second rest

Dumbbell Workouts

Arm Day

These are the workouts I like to use to work out my arms! I normally start each workout with a 30 minute cardio session (I prefer using the elliptical). I do 5 sets with 10 repetitions for each arm.

  • Bicep curls
  • Over-the-head press
  • Tricep extension
  • Rest

Next I do 5 sets with 10 repetitions for each arm with these workouts:

  • Lateral raise
  • Front raise
  • Another front raise but slightly more outward
  • Push-ups
  • Rest

Leg Day

Leg day is my favorite! Here is the first set of workouts I do after my cardio! Again, I do 5 sets with 10 repetitions each all with dumbells!

  • Squats
  • Sumo squats
  • Front lunges
  • Kettle bell swing
  • Rest

The next set has the same repetitions but no dumbells (you can add resistance bands if you are feeling up to it!

  • Fire hydrants
  • Bent knee hip extensions
  • Hip thrusts
  • Rest

Cardio Workouts

Track Workout

A lot of people do not enjoy running, but this short, but effective cardio workout will make you feel good afterwards. I like to do a quick core workout after the run to finish off the workout.

  • Find a track and do a 5 minute warm up jog or walk
  • Sprint the straight ends of the tracks and walk or jog the curves
  • Repeat this for 15 minutes
  • Do a cooldown walk for 5 minutes
  • OPTIONAL: add in a core workout of your choice

Treadmill Workout

If it is a rainy day but you are still motivated to get a workout in, head to the gym and find a treadmill!

  • Do a 5 minute warm up walk or jog
  • Sprint for 30 seconds
  • Walk or jog for 30 seconds
  • Repeat for 15 minutes
  • Cooldown for 5 minutes
  • OPTIONAL: If you want to add more of a challenge, put your treadmill on an incline
