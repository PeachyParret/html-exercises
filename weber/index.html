Food

Blacksburg has a long list of iconic restaurants that every visitor should check out if given the opportunity. However, some are more budget-friendly than others. If you feel like going out, or just don't want to cook, check out these popular stops that are a little easier on the wallet:

  • Green's Grill and Sushi Bar: If you're trying to go out with a group of people that can't agree on one kind of food, Green's is a great place to go. Green's is popular for sushi, but it also serves Mediterranean food, tacos, vegetarian fare, and more.
  • Mellow Mushroom: Mellow Mushroom isn't a Blacksburg-exclusive restaurant, but it can often be cheaper than going to a chain pizza place (depending on what you get, of course).
  • Chinese Kitchen: Not necessarily a restaurant, but if you're in the mood for Chinese takeout, this is a great spot. I'm personally a fan of the crabmeat fried rice :)

Housing

Housing in Blacksburg is getting pretty close to an oxymoron. Still, there's some relatively inexpensive options out there for you. Something to keep in mind is that the further away from Blacksburg you get, the cheaper it is, but that might translate to higher transportation costs if it means you need to drive to get to campus. Check out some of these apartment complexes:

  • Shawnee
  • Chasewood Downs
  • The Mill

This is clearly not a comprehensive list, but my general advice is to look as far away from campus as you can, but opt for a place that has bus stops nearby. This ensures that you aren't paying for proximity, but you can still get to where you need to go easily.

Transportation

Transportation in Blacksburg is by no means perfect, but there's definitely some options to work with:

  • Blacksburg Transit is by and large what most of the population uses to get around Virginia Tech's campus and its neighboring housing areas. It's free, and bus stops are located throughout Blacksburg. The Blacksburg Transit app allows you to see where the buses are in real time, and view the schedules for each bus.
  • If you don't own a car and you're looking to get out of town, there's a couple Virginia Tech carpooling Facebook groups that might be able to help you out. That said, be prepared to chip in for gas.

Entertainment

One convenient aspect about Blacksburg is that it has a plethora of ways to have an inexpensive, fun time. Check out the following:

  • GobblerConnect: GobblerConnect hosts a calendar for most of Virginia Tech associated events, including club meetings, craft groups, musical performances, and more. Many of these events offer free food for participants as well :)
  • Nextthreedays: Nextthreedays is essentially the New River Valley equivalent of Gobblerconnect, except it's focused on, as it says in the name, events in the next three days.
  • The Lyric Theatre: The Lyric offers a free showing of a particular film each Friday. Show times may vary.
  • The Blacksburg Farmers Market: The Blacksburg Farmers Market often features live music and plenty of dogs to fawn over. The market is year round, though vendors may vary depending on the season. Hours are also subject to change, so make sure you check the website here before you go.

Virginia Tech, Blacksburg, and Montgomery County Resources

It can be hard to figure out where to start if you want information on financial resources, but I've compiled some that I and people I know personally have found helpful.

  • If you're a Virginia Tech student, make sure to check out the financial aid portal here
  • Virginia Tech further maintains a Student Emergency Fund, which students can apply for if they are suddenly put in a dire financial situation.
  • The Market at Virginia Tech is a food pantry exclusively for Virginia Tech students.
  • Hokie Wellness maintains a list of financial resources for anyone to access.

Virginia and Federal Resources

Virginia offers a myriad of resources to support its residents. They are as follows

  • You can find out if you're eligible for SNAP benefits here
  • If you've recently lost your health insurance, check out this Enroll Virginia! Fact Sheet
  • Virginia offers a child care subsidy program for children who meet eligibility requirements.
  • You can apply for several assistance programs, including energy assistance, auxiliary grants, temporary cash assistance for families with minor children (TANF), and more, here.
  • Feeding America lists food banks across the U.S.
  • Find out if you're eligible for Virginia's Special Supplemental Nutrition program for Women, Infants, and Children.
  • You can apply for unemployment benefits here
  • Certain counties and localities in Virginia may have additional resources. Make sure to visit your local government websites to keep up to date on other programs they may offer.
