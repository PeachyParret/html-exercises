Where to shop sustainably

The first step to committing to be earth-friendly is ditching big corporations as much as you can. When you shop at places like Amazon, Target, Walmart, Trader Joe's, and Costco, you are supporting poorer working conditions, often unethical treatment of animals, high carbon emmissions, and more. Shopping small at businesses that support environmentalism not only eases your conscience but also helps the planet in a big way. Take it from us, the first step is to start finding good websites that focus on reducing our carbon footprint while keeping prices competitive with large corporations. Here are some sites that we love... and you should, too.

Ecoternatives

"We've reviewed 1,400+ products to curate a selection of the most affordable eco-goods without sacrificing on sustainability or product quality, and we're making change."

EarthHero

"We started EarthHero with the ultimate goal of helping to build a more sustainable world by allowing consumers to vote for (and love) sustainable products. While this approach is an area we can comfortably grow into, we’re not interested in being comfortable, we’re interested in being better. Moving forward, we aim to increase our environmental and sustainability educational content, use our platform to lift marginalized voices, spotlight and learn from traditional knowledge experts, and publicly support environmental causes and legislation. Sustainability should not be accessible only to those with resources, but about making resources available to all."

EcoRoots

"Here at EcoRoots, we’re on an endless mission to provide you with the best eco-friendly and low-waste products out there. Our products are kindly made for every person, every body, every identity. From beauty and health to household products, our eco-friendly lineup is here to help you find sustainable alternatives to everyday essentials!"

Public Goods

"Many of the toughest problems we face are the result of the products big companies produce. That’s why we’re committed to making healthier, more sustainable choices easy and accessible to all."

Grove Collaborative

"We're here to empower a sustainable revolution in your home. Through our marketplace of 200+ vetted brands, we're committed to reducing and offsetting plastic waste, establishing strict ingredients standards, and crafting our own planet-first products to lead the way."
